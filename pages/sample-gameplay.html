Sample Gameplay: Skirmish at the Clockwork Nexus

Scenario Overview

  • Battle Size: 150 points per player
  • Player Commander: Lord Calculon (Knowledge Evolution)
  • Opponent Commander: Lady Cogsworth (Chaos Evolution)

Step 1 — Army Setup

Lord Calculon's Army (Iron Dominion, 150 points)

Commander: Lord Calculon (Level 10, Knowledge Evolution)

Units:

  • 10 Clockwork Infantry (20 points)
  • 5 Elite Vanguard (20 points)
  • 4 Gearwright Engineers (12 points)
  • 6 Steam Artillery Crew (30 points)
  • 5 Clockwork Cavalry (20 points)

War Machines:

  • 1 Clockwork Titan (12 points)
  • 1 Steam Colossus (10 points)

Fragment Assignments:

  • Core Fragment (minor buff to all units)
  • Overclock Node (enhances war machine activation)

Lady Cogsworth's Army (Iron Dominion, 150 points)

Commander: Lady Cogsworth (Level 10, Chaos Evolution)

Units:

  • 8 Clockwork Cavalry (32 points)
  • 6 Steam Recon Flyers (18 points)
  • 5 Aether Infused Soldiers (15 points)
  • 4 Experimental Constructs (16 points)

War Machines:

  • 1 Chrono Walker (14 points)
  • 1 Arcane Steam Golem (11 points)

Fragment Assignments:

  • Fragment Swarm (may warp battlefield)
  • Reality Shard (random terrain alteration)

Step 2 — Tactical Layer Setup

Battlefield: Industrial Clockwork Nexus

Terrain Hazards: Steam vents, conveyor belts, fragment shards

Commander Cards Available

  • Calculon: Coordinated Strike (precision attacks), Overclocked Engines (war machine efficiency)
  • Cogsworth: Unstable Skirmisher (random AoE), Fragment Conduit (warped battlefield)

Step 3 — Sample Turn Sequence

Turn 1

  • Calculon deploys Clockwork Infantry at central corridor; activates Core Fragment (stable +1 attack)
  • Lady Cogsworth moves Cavalry flanking; triggers Fragment Swarm, causing unpredictable AoE nearby

Turn 2

  • Calculon uses Overclock Node → Clockwork Titan attacks enemy rear; damage +2
  • Lady Cogsworth activates Reality Shard → terrain shift causes Calculon's Cavalry to lose movement this turn

Turn 3 — Tactical Zoom-In

  • Calculon's Elite Vanguard confronts Cogsworth's Experimental Constructs
  • Fragment interaction: Knowledge Evolution → precise bonus → constructs take extra damage, Calculon's units remain stable
  • Cogsworth's Chaos Evolution: Experimental Constructs trigger random AoE → minor friendly collateral damage

Turn 4

  • Clockwork Titan smashes through enemy lines, AoE hit on 2 units
  • Chrono Walker warps activation order; Calculon's Gearwright Engineers delayed next turn

Turn 5

  • Calculon coordinates Clockwork Cavalry + Elite Vanguard using Coordinated Strike card → double attacks on priority target
  • Cogsworth uses Unstable Skirmisher → Fragment interaction causes minor terrain shift, boosts speed of remaining Cavalry

Step 4 — Outcome / Notes

  • Calculon's Knowledge evolution provided stable, predictable damage and tactical advantage
  • Cogsworth's Chaos evolution introduced high-risk battlefield changes, creating unpredictability and excitement
  • Fragments directly influenced battle flow: Core Fragment, Overclock Node, Fragment Swarm, Reality Shard
  • Tactical zoom-ins added emergent gameplay, showing RPG-style skills and fragment effects in action
  • Multi-day campaign continuation: surviving units retain buffs/damage, XP awarded to commanders for skill upgrades

Step 5 — Lessons / Tips for Players

  • Commander evolution matters: Knowledge favors control; Chaos favors risk/reward; Hybrid balances both
  • Fragment choice is strategic: Combining multiple fragments can amplify effects but increases risk
  • Unit selection: Align units with commander's style and signature units for maximum synergy
  • Tactical zoom-ins: Elite units and fragment events can turn the tide of battle
  • Point scaling: Use the army builder to adjust points for skirmishes or epic multi-day campaigns
