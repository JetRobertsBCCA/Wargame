## Prompt Modifiers & Style Guide

Every prompt in this file follows a consistent structure. Use these notes to understand, modify, or extend them.

### Prompt Structure
Each prompt follows this order:
1. **Medium/Style** — "Digital concept art, [type] illustration, dark fantasy [faction style]"
2. **Subject** — What the image shows (creature, warrior, machine, etc.)
3. **Physical Details** — Body type, height, skin, scars, hair, mutations, etc.
4. **Armor/Clothing** — Specific materials, textures, colors, motifs, wear/damage
5. **Weapons/Equipment** — Exact weapon types, sizes, materials, effects
6. **Pose/Action** — What the subject is doing, body language, stance
7. **Special Effects** — Glows, energy, fire, cosmic distortion, smoke, etc.
8. **Background/Setting** — Environment, battlefield context, weather, terrain
9. **Lighting** — Direction, color temperature, mood-setting light sources
10. **Composition** — Camera angle, framing, what should be prominent
11. **Quality Tags** — "Highly detailed, [descriptor], [palette note], 4K"

### Key Principles
- **No game jargon** — Replace all game-mechanic terms with visual equivalents ("Stage 2 Blight" → describe the physical mutation)
- **Front-load the subject** — AI generators weight early words more heavily
- **Concrete over abstract** — "6-foot-tall", "burnished brass", "3-inch fangs" instead of "large", "metallic", "fearsome"
- **Named colors** — "Crimson red", "midnight blue", "burnished gold" not "red", "blue", "gold"
- **Specific materials** — "Rough-hammered iron with visible forge-marks" not "metal armor"
- **Action over description** — Show the character doing something, not just standing
- **One focal point** — Each prompt has one primary visual focus even in group shots
